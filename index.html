Akifumi Wachi

About Me

I am a research scientist at IBM Research AI. Before joining IBM, I was a graduate student in Department of Aeronautics and Astronautics at the University of Tokyo. During my master's degree, I participated in the development or operation of small satellites (e.g., EQUULEUS).

My Japanese name is 和地 瞭良. I like reading, traveling, and eating.

Research Interests

My research interests are in Machine Learning, Autonomous Control, and Robotics. Especially, I have been working on:

  • Safe Reinforcement Learnig
  • Multi-agent Reinforcement Learning
  • Adversarial Reinforcement Learning

More specifically, I aim to apply reinforcement learning to create a reliable and safer society. For example, in a recent publication (IJCAI-19), I proposed a method to test an autonomous driving car by training other cars in an adversarial way using multi-agent reinforcement learning.
